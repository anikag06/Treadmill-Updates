How to play?

Click on the happy faces as quickly as you can.

Don't click on the non-happy faces.


Science behind the game.

It has been shown that depressed people have very poor self-esteem. Their self-esteem is dependent on other people's approval. Let's say a depressed person is talking to a group of friends, and he cracks a joke. If some of his friends don't laugh at his joke, he will forget about the rest of his interactions with his friends and focus just on this fact that some of his friends didn't laugh at his joke. He might have thoughts like "My friends think I am stupid" based on this. He will ignore the fact that this was a one-time event and that some of his friends did laugh at his joke. One of the reasons why this happens is that depressed individuals tend to selectively focus on negative information and ignore positive information. This bias for negative information has a further negative impact on the person's self-esteem.
The "Identify the friendly face" game is supposed to modify this bias for negative information. It is supposed to train depressed individuals to focus on positive information and ignore negative information. We expect it to help improve their self-esteem and eventually with depression. But, you have to do it from time to time. You can't just do it once or twice and leave it.
