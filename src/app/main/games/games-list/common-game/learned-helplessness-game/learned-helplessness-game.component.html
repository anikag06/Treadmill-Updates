Something very interesting just happened. You gave up on a level that you were able to solve earlier. One explanation for that is Learned Helplessness.

What is Learned Helplessness?

When we fail in one area of our lives, be it career or relationships, we start giving up in other areas of our lives as well. This phenomenon is called Learned Helplessness.

Learned Helplessness is a model for depression. People who are more likely to develop Learned Helplessness are more likely to get depressed.

How is this game related to Learned Helplessness?

This game was designed to give you an intuitive feel for Learned Helplessness. The first set of games were solvable. The next set of games were not solvable and was designed to make you give up. After giving up on this unsolvable game, you gave up on a level you had solved earlier.

What can I learn from this game?

We develop Learned Helplessness when we think that we are responsible for our failure. But often there are other factors beyond our control. When you fail in anything, don't immediately jump to the conclusion that it was completely your fault. Check if there were other factors beyond your control that influenced the results. When facing a new situation, give it your best shot. Don't give up quickly because you think "There is no point in trying, I am going to fail anyway."
