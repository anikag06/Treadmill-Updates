Types of thinking errors
1. Overgeneralization

You tend to generalize one negative thing that happened to you to all parts of your life.

2. Labeling

You assign yourself or others a fixed, generalized label based on a few instances.

3. Magnification

You magnify the negative aspects of yourself, a situation, or a person while ignoring the positive aspects.

4. Minimization

You minimize the positive aspects of yourself, a situation, or a person while ignoring the positive aspects.

5. Ignoring the positive

You ignore anything positive about yourself.

6. All-or-none thinking

You think in extremes and avoid taking any middle ground. You divide everything in either perfect or absolute crap. There is nothing called “good enough” in your dictionary

7. Self-blame

You believe that you are the cause of all the bad things happening to you. Everything that is going wrong is your fault.

8. Should statement

You know how you or others should behave, and when these criteria are not met, you can't stand it.

9. Mental filter

You focus on one negative aspect rather than seeing the complete picture.

10. Emotional reasoning

You assume your thought is true just because you feel it is true. You ignore any and all evidence to the contrary.

11. Jumping to conclusion

Mind reading - You are sure you know what others are thinking. Catastrophizing - You are sure that the worst possible outcome will occur.
